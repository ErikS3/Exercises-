				<p>As the fight to win the 2016 presidential election heats up, here is a guide to who may be the next president of the United States:</p>

<h3><strong>Republicans</strong></h3>
<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/jeb-bush" target="_blank">Jeb Bush</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:left;padding:0 8px 0 0;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815jeb-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>John Ellis Bush (his name "JEB" is actually an acronym) served as governor of Florida from 1999 to 2007, after narrowly missing winning that job in 1994. Bush is known for his moderate stances on immigration and Common Core, although he says the federal government should have no role in the Common Core debate. He's the brother of former President George W. Bush and the son of former President George H.W. Bush. After his time as governor, he founded the Foundation for Excellence in Education, which is now chaired by former Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Has a healthcare plan that would repeal Obamacare and replace it with new health insurance tax credits.</p>

<p>Religion: Roman Catholic (converted from Episcopalian)</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/ben-carson" target="_blank">Ben Carson</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Ben%20carson-ap.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Carson is a retired neurosurgeon who rose to political fame after speaking at the National Prayer Breakfast in 2013. Although he has never held elective office, Carson is a published author, serves on the board of multiple corporations, and received the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2008 for his medical work. Carson is an outspoken opponent of Obamacare. He also has a history of controversial comments, including that Obamacare is the "worst thing … since slavery."</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

					<div id="ad_2564959_8" class="ad dynamic-load article-ad" data-targeting="keywords:beltway confidential|opinion|president|republican party|democratic party|joe biden|hillary clinton|2016 elections|campaigns|minusextra|jeb bush|jason russell,section:opinion,subsection:beltway-confidential" data-position="8" data-desktop-sizes="[[728,90],[300,250]]" data-desktop-placement="" data-tablet-sizes="[[728,90],[300,250]]" data-tablet-placement="" data-mobile-sizes="[300,250]" data-mobile-placement=""></div>
				

<p>• Wants to ratify a Balanced Budget Amendment to the Constitution.</p>

<p>• Wants to keep the detention camp at Guantanamo Bay open.</p>

<p>• Opposes any federal funding for Planned Parenthood.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Carson launched his campaign with a speech in Detroit on May 4.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (fourth)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in fifth place), Sept. 16 main debate (fourth), Oct. 28 main debate (second), Nov. 10 main debate (second), Dec. 15 main debate (second), Jan. 14 main debate (fourth), Jan. 28 main debate (second) and only Feb. 6 debate (fourth)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 65</p>

<p>Education: Yale University (Psychology), University of Michigan (M.D.)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Candy), three children (Murray, Benjamin, Rhoeyce)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Detroit, Mich.</p>

<p>Current Residence: West Friendship, Md.</p>

<p>Religion: Seventh-Day Adventist</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/chris-christie" target="_blank">Chris Christie</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:left;padding:0 8px 0 0;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Christie-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Christie has been the governor of New Jersey since 2010. Prior to that, he was the U.S. attorney for the District of New Jersey from 2001 to 2008. Although he was cleared from wrongdoing in the infmaous "Bridgegate" scandal, Christie's reputation still suffers from it. Christie is well known for having a moderate-to-conservative record in deep-blue New Jersey and vehemently defending that record against hecklers, including teachers union members. Christie said "the national teachers union" deserves a punch in the face in an August 2015 interview.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to reform entitlement programs, including partial means-testing of Social Security for future retirees earning over $80,000.</p>

<p>• Wants to undo the sequestration budget cuts on defense.</p>

<p>• Wants a border wall "where appropriate," especially near Southern cities.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Christie launched his campaign on June 30 in a rally at a high school gym in Livingston, N.J., where Christie grew up.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (10th)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in ninth place), Sept. 16 main debate (10th) and Oct. 28 main debate (eighth). Did not qualify for Nov. 10 main debate (ninth). Qualified for Dec. 15 main debate (seventh), Jan. 14 main debate (fifth), Jan. 28 main debate (sixth) and only Feb. 6 debate (sixth)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 54</p>

<p>Education: University of Delaware (Political Science), Seton Hall University (J.D.)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Mary Pat), four children (Andrew, Sarah, Patrick, Bridget)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Newark, N.J.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Mendham, N.J.</p>

<p>Religion: Roman Catholic</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/ted-cruz" target="_blank">Ted Cruz</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Cruz-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Cruz became a U.S. senator from Texas in 2013, when a delayed election calendar helped him get his message out and defeat an establishment Republican candidate who outspent him three-to-one. He was a partner at Morgan Lewis law firm and solicitor general for the state of Texas. He has successfully argued cases before the Supreme Court. Cruz was criticized by some and became a hero to others by trying to defund Obamacare during budget negotiations in Fall 2013 that ended in a government shutdown. Cruz was born in Canada, but he was born to an American mother, so he is most likely still eligible for the presidency.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Opposes the Export-Import Bank.</p>

<p>• Opposes ethanol subsidies through the renewable fuel standard.</p>

<p>• Wants states to be able to have their own definitions of marriage.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Cruz formally announced his presidential campaign in a speech at Liberty University on March 30, the earliest of all the candidates.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (first)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in sixth place), Sept. 16 main debate (fifth), Oct. 28 main debate (sixth), Nov. 10 main debate (fourth), Dec. 15 main debate (third), Jan. 14 main debate (second), Jan. 28 main debate (second) and only Feb. 6 debate (second)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 45</p>

<p>Education: Princeton University (Public Policy), Harvard Law School (J.D.)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Heidi), two children (Caroline, Catherine)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Calgary, Canada (Cruz's mother was born in the United States, so he is still eligible for the presidency)</p>

<p>Current Residence: Houston, Texas</p>

<p>Religion: Southern Baptist</p>

<h4><strong><a target="_blank" href="Carly%20Fiorina">Carly Fiorina</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:left;padding:0 8px 0 0;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815fiorina-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Fiorina was CEO of Hewlett-Packard from 1999 to 2005. She is the only Republican woman in the race. Fiorina is the chairman of the American Conservative Union Foundation, which perhaps helps her credibility with Tea Partiers. She has never held elective office. She ran for U.S. Senate in California in 2010, losing 52 to 42 percent to the Democratic incumbent, Sen. Barbara Boxer.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Supports the DREAM Act, which would allow some undocumented immigrants to earn permanent legal residency, but wants to secure the border first.</p>

<p>• Wants to arm Ukraine to fight rebels backed by Russians.</p>

<p>• Wants to cut federal tax revenue, but has yet to produce a specific plan on it.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Fiorina announced she was running in an interview with Good Morning America on May 4 and held an online town hall later that day.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (seventh)</p>

<p>Debates: Did not qualify for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 14th place). Qualified for Sept. 16 main debate (11th), Oct. 28 main debate (fourth), Nov. 10 main debate (sixth) and Dec. 15 main debate (sixth). Did not quality for Jan. 14 main debate (ninth) or Jan. 28 main debate (10th). Did not qualify for the Feb. 6 debate (eighth)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 62</p>

<p>Education: Stanford University (Medieval History and Philosophy), UCLA Law School (did not finish), University of Maryland (MBA), Massachusetts Institute of Technology Sloan School of Management (Master of Science)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Frank), two step-children (Lori Ann [deceased], Traci)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Austin, Texas</p>

<p>Current Residence: Mason Neck, Va.</p>

<p>Religion: Christian</p>

<h4><strong>Jim Gilmore</strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/072715-getty-gilmore1.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Gilmore served as governor of Virginia from 1998 to 2002 (term limits prevented another run). Gilmore also ran for president in the 2008 cycle, but withdrew after six months. Instead he ran for the U.S. Senate, losing by 21 points to now-Senator Mark Warner. As governor, Gilmore phased out property taxes on vehicles and cut spending during the early 2000s recession. Gilmore is also a veteran of the U.S. Army and was chairman of the Republican National Committee in 2001.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to repeal and replace Obamacare with a healthcare bill that would allow insurance to be sold across state lines, among other reforms.</p>

<p>• Believes climate change is real but opposes Obama's methods, including cap and trade, to fix it.</p>

<p>• Seems to support deportation of all illegal immigrants with no path to citizenship.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Gilmore launched his candidacy on July 30 in a YouTube video, the last of the 17 candidates.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (12th)</p>

<p>Debates: Did not qualify for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 17th place). Did not qualify for either Sept. 16 debate (17th), either Oct. 28 debate (15th), either Nov. 10 debate (15th), either Dec. 15 debate (14th) or either Jan. 14 debate (12th). Did not qualify for Jan. 28 main debate (11th). Did not qualify for the Feb. 6 debate (ninth)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 67</p>

<p>Education: University of Virginia (undergraduate and law school)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Roxane), two children</p>

<p>Birthplace: Richmond, Va.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Alexandria, Va., or Richmond, Va.</p>

<p>Religion: Methodist</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/lindsey-graham" target="_blank">Lindsey Graham</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. Graham suspended his campaign in a video message released Dec. 21, saying many of the other candidates had adopted his defense policy views. During his campaign, Graham never received more than two percent support in a national, Iowa or New Hampshire poll.</p>

<p>Endorsed: Jeb Bush</p>

<p>Debates: Did not qualify for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 15th place), Sept. 16 main debate (16th) or Oct. 28 main debate (14th). Did not qualify for either Nov. 10 debate (13th). Did not qualify for Dec. 15 main debate (13th)</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/mike-huckabee" target="_blank">Mike Huckabee</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Huckabee-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Huckabee served as governor of Arkansas from 1996 to 2007. Following his failed campaign for the GOP presidential nomination in 2008, he hosted the "Huckabee" show on Fox News until early 2015. Huckabee is well-known for his very conservative positions on social and cultural issues, and was an ordained Southern Baptist minister prior to becoming Arkansas' lieutenant governor. Huckabee is critical of some conservative economic policies, such as reforming Social Security.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to eliminate the income tax (and the IRS) and replace it with a 23 percent federal sales tax, also known as the FairTax.</p>

<p>• Wants a unified Jerusalem.</p>

<p>• Wants to "kill" Common Core and abolish the federal Department of Education.</p>

<p>Status: Dropped out. Huckabee ended his campaign on Feb. 1, after a poor finish in the Iowa Caucuses. After winning there in 2008, Huckabee came 10th in 2016. May 2015 was the last time Huckabee earned double-digit support in a national primary poll.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (ninth)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in fourth place), Sept. 16 main debate (seventh) and Oct. 28 main debate (seventh). Did not qualify for Nov. 10 main debate (ninth), Dec. 15 main debate (10th), Jan. 14 main debate (10th) or Jan. 28 main debate (eighth)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 61</p>

<p>Education: Ouachita Baptist University (Religion), Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary (did not finish)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Janet), three children (John Mark, David, Sarah)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Hope, Ark.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Santa Rosa Beach, Fla.</p>

<p>Religion: Southern Baptist</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/bobby-jindal" target="_blank">Bobby Jindal</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. Jindal suspended his campaign on Nov. 17 in a Fox News interview. After entering the race, Jindal never received more than two percent in a national or New Hampshire primary poll. He peaked at seven percent in one Iowa poll.</p>

<p>Endorsed: Marco Rubio</p>

<p>Debates: Did not qualify for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 13th place), Sept. 16 main debate (14th), Oct. 28 main debate (12th) or Nov. 10 main debate (12th)</p>

<h4><strong><a target="_blank" href="John%20Kasich">John Kasich</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815john-kasich-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Kasich has served as the governor of Ohio since 2011. Kasich had an 18-year stay in Congress until he decided against re-election, instead trying a presidential bid that didn't last beyond the 2000 Iowa Straw Poll. Early in his time as governor, Kasich signed a law strictly limiting collective bargaining rights, but the law was overturned in a referendum. Kasich has been criticized by conservatives for expanding Medicaid in Ohio through Obamacare, and also for his support for Common Core.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to lift the budget sequestration caps on military spending, while cutting waste in the Pentagon.</p>

<p>• Wants all health insurance regulation to be done by states.</p>

<p>• Supports ending federal funding for Planned Parenthood, but doesn't want to shut down the government over it.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Kasich launched his campaign on July 21 with a rally at his college alma mater in Columbus, Ohio.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (eighth)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 10th place), Sept. 16 main debate (ninth), Oct. 28 main debate (eighth), Nov. 10 main debate (seventh), Dec. 15 main debate (seventh), Jan. 14 main debate (eighth), Jan. 28 main debate (seventh) and Feb. 6 debate (seventh)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 64</p>

<p>Education: Ohio State (Political Science)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Karen), two children (Emma, Reese)</p>

<p>Birthplace: McKees Rocks, Pa.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Westerville, Ohio</p>

<p>Religion: Anglican (converted from Catholicism)</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/george-pataki" target="_blank">George Pataki</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. Pataki started calling supporters in New Hampshire on Dec. 29 to tell them he was ending his campaign. Pataki never received more than three percent in a national poll, one percent in an Iowa poll or two percent in a New Hampshire poll.</p>

<p>Endorsed: Marco Rubio</p>

<p>Debates: Did not qualify for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 16th place), Sept. 16 main debate (15th) or Oct. 28 main debate (12th). Did not qualify for either Nov. 10 debate (14th). Did not qualify for Dec. 15 main debate (12th)</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/rand-paul" target="_blank">Rand Paul</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Rand-paul-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Paul has been a senator from Kentucky since 2011, after Tea Party support helped him upset an establishment party-favorite Republican in the primary. The son of a former presidential candidate, former Rep. Ron Paul, R-Texas, Paul already has a solid base of libertarian support. He was cheered by conservatives in 2013 for his 13-hour filibuster of now-CIA Director John Brennan's nomination, motivated by President Obama's drone strike policy. Paul has campaigned on "trying to kill the tax code," preferring dramatically simpler and lower taxes.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to simplify the personal income tax into one rate of 14.5 percent, with a standard deduction of $15,000 and a personal exemption of $5,000.</p>

<p>• Would seek Congressional approval before sending troops "into harm's way."</p>

<p>• Wants to audit the Federal Reserve and place its regulatory power back with Congress.</p>

<p>Status: Dropped out. Paul announced he was ending his campaign on Feb. 3, saying "I will continue to carry the torch for Liberty in the United States Senate and I look forward to earning the privilege to represent the people of Kentucky for another term." Paul led some national and New Hampshire GOP polls in 2013 and 2014 but lost traction in 2015.</p>

<p>Endorsed: Paul's staff says he won't endorse anyone during the primary, but he plans to endorse the eventual GOP nominee.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (fifth)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in eighth place), Sept. 16 main debate (eighth), Oct. 28 main debate (eighth), Nov. 10 main debate (eighth) and Dec. 15 main debate (seventh). Did not qualify for Jan. 14 main debate (seventh) and declined to participate in the undercard. Qualified for Jan. 28 main debate (eighth)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 53</p>

<p>Education: Baylor University (did not finish), Duke University School of Medicine (M.D.)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Kelley), three children (Robert, Duncan, William)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Pittsburgh, Pa.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Bowling Green, Ky.</p>

<p>Religion: Presbyterian (converted from Episcopalian)</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/rick-perry" target="_blank">Rick Perry</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. Perry ended his campaign on September 11 in a speech in St. Louis, making him the first candidate to drop out. It was reported that Perry's campaign had laid off much of its staff and was running low on funds.</p>

<p>Endorsed: Ted Cruz</p>

<p>Debates: Did not qualify for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 11th place) or Sept. 16 main debate (12th)</p>

<h4><strong><a target="_blank" href="Marco%20Rubio">Marco Rubio</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:left;padding:0 8px 0 0;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Rubio-AP.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Rubio has been a senator from Florida since 2011, after Tea Party support helped push him past Charlie Crist in a three-way general election. Prior to that, Rubio had a nine-year career in the Florida House of Representatives, two years of which he was speaker of the House. Rubio's Cuban descent has been a notable influence on his support for conservative immigration reform and on Cuban-American relations, an issue he has criticized Obama heavily on.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to reform the tax code into two brackets at 15 percent and 35 percent. Would eliminate many tax credits and deductions but create an additional child tax credit of $2,500.</p>

<p>• Wants to repeal Obamacare and replace it with refundable tax credits for purchasing health insurance. Would let insurance be sold across state lines.</p>

<p>• Wants to undo the sequestration budget cuts on defense and use funds to "modernize our forces."</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Rubio formally announced his presidential campaign in a speech on April 13 in Miami, Fla.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (third)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in sixth place), Sept. 16 main debate (sixth), Oct. 28 main debate (third), Nov. 10 main debate (third), Dec. 15 main debate (fourth), Jan. 14 main debate (third), Jan. 28 main debate (third) and Feb. 6 debate (third)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 45</p>

<p>Education: University of Florida (Political Science), University of Miami School of Law (J.D.)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Jeanette), four children (Amanda Loren, Daniella, Anthony, Dominick)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Miami, Fla.</p>

<p>Current Residence: West Miami, Fla.</p>

<p>Religion: Roman Catholic</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/rick-santorum" target="_blank">Rick Santorum</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Santorum-Getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Santorum was a U.S. senator from Pennsylvania from 1995 to 2007. He is well-known for his 2012 presidential campaign, when he arguably finished second in the GOP primaries and won the Iowa Caucuses. Santorum has been the CEO of EchoLight Studios since 2013, a film company that produces Christian films. He is most famous for his very conservative positions on social issues, like a ban on most abortions.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to withhold federal funds from sanctuary cities that refuse to cooperate with federal authorities. Also wants to reduce legal immigration by 25 percent.</p>

<p>• Wants to reform taxes into 20 percent rates on individual and business income, while dramatically cutting the size of the IRS.</p>

<p>• Thinks the Iran deal should be treated like a treaty, and be rejected by the Senate.</p>

<p>Status: Dropped out. Santorum announced he was ending his campaign on Feb. 3 after a poor Iowa finish. Santorum is the only candidate to participate in every undercard debate. Since the start of 2015, he never received more than six percent support nationally, in Iowa or in New Hampshire.</p>

<p>Endorsed: Marco Rubio</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (11th)</p>

<p>Debates: Did not qualify for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in 12th place), Sept. 16 main debate (13th), Oct. 28 main debate (11th), Nov. 10 main debate (11th), Dec. 15 main debate (11th), Jan. 14 main debate (11th) or Jan. 28 main debate (11th)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 58</p>

<p>Education: Penn State University (Political Science), University of Pittsburgh (M.B.A.), Dickinson School of Law (J.D.)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Karen), eight children (Elizabeth, Johnny, Daniel, Gabriel [deceased], Sarah, Peter, Patrick, Bella)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Winchester, Va.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Great Falls, Va., or Dallas, Texas</p>

<p>Religion: Roman Catholic</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/donald-trump" target="_blank">Donald Trump</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:left;padding:0 8px 0 0;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/trump-2016guide.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Trump is the son of Fred Trump, a wealthy real estate developer. Donald Trump is a successful businessman in his own right. Trump has flirted with running in the past and appeared at multiple Conservative Political Action Conferences. His brash personality and wealth make him one of the most well-known figures in the country. Although he was a Republican before 1999, Trump was a registered Democrat from 2001-2009 and previously supported universal healthcare. He was the host of "The Apprentice," a reality show on NBC.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to reform taxes into four brackets at zero, 10, 20, and 25 percent, while phasing out all deductions except for the charitable and mortgage interest deductions.</p>

<p>• In response to mass gun tragedies, wants to expand treatment programs for people with mental health problems.</p>

<p>• Wants to build a wall across the Mexican border, and would increase fees on border crossing cards, temporary visas and entry at border ports to pay for it.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Trump launched his campaign on June 16 at the Trump Tower in New York City. He delivered remarks, some off-the-cuff, to a room full of supporters. Trump's comments about Mexican immigrants were controversial: "They're bringing drugs, they're bringing crime, they're rapists, and some, I assume, are good people."</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (second)</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in first place), Sept. 16 main debate (first), Oct. 28 main debate (first), Nov. 10 main debate (first), Dec. 15 main debate (first) and Jan. 14 main debate (first). Qualified for Jan. 28 main debate (first) but declined to participate due to feud with moderator Megyn Kelly. Qualified for Feb. 6 debate (first)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 70</p>

<p>Education: Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania (Economics)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Ivana [divorced], Marla [divorced], Melania), five children (Donald, Ivanka, Eric, Tiffany, Barron)</p>

<p>Birthplace: New York, N.Y.</p>

<p>Current Residence: New York, N.Y.</p>

<p>Religion: Presbyterian</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/scott-walker" target="_blank">Scott Walker</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. Walker suspended his campaign on September 21 with a press conference in Madison, Wis. Some of Walker's major donors had announced they would start giving money to other candidates, and others had doubts about his campaign's leadership.</p>

<p>Debates: Qualified for Aug. 6 main debate (polled in third place) and Sept. 16 main debate (third)</p>

<h3><strong>Democrats</strong></h3>
<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/joe-biden" target="_blank">Joe Biden</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Not going to run. After months of speculation, Biden announced on Oct. 21 that he would not seek the Democratic nomination for president. But Biden said he would "speak out clearly and forcefully, to influence as much as I can where we stand as a party and where we need to go as a nation." He commented on several issues that he likely would have campaigned on if he were running, including a cancer cure, income inequality and free public college.</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/lincoln-chafee" target="_blank">Lincoln Chafee</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. Chafee withdrew from the campaign on Oct. 23, in a speech at the National Issues Conference of the Women's Leadership Forum. Chafee's name was mispronounced as he took the stage. He received more than 2 percent support in just three national, Iowa or New Hampshire primary polls.</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/hillary-clinton" target="_blank">Hillary Clinton</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:right;padding:0 0 0 8px;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815hillary-getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Clinton is the best-known candidate in the race. Voters are familiar with her from her time as secretary of state, 2008 presidential campaign and time in the U.S. Senate. Her husband is obviously pretty famous too. It remains to be seen how her lesser-known controversies will affect her White House bid. Clinton has been accused of corruption for allowing the Clinton Foundation to accept donations from foreign governments and hiding emails from when she was secretary of state.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Opposes the Trans-Pacific Partnership free trade agreement.</p>

<p>• Opposes Keystone XL pipeline.</p>

<p>• Wants to raise taxes on capital gains held for one to six years.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Clinton officially launched her campaign on April 12 in a video message.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (first)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 69</p>

<p>Education: Wellesley College (Political Science), Yale University Law School (J.D.)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Bill), one child (Chelsea)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Chicago, Ill.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Chappaqua, N.Y. or Washington, D.C.</p>

<p>Religion: Methodist</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/martin-omalley" target="_blank">Martin O'Malley</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. After a very poor finish in Iowa, Martin O'Malley decided to end his campaign on Feb. 1. He got less than one percent support in the Iowa Caucuses. In more than two years of polling the Democratic presidential primary, O'Malley never reached double-digit support in national polls.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (third)</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/bernie-sanders" target="_blank">Bernie Sanders</a></strong></h4>
<div style="float:left;padding:0 8px 0 0;font-size:11px;color:dimgray;margin-bottom:8px;"><img alt="" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/content.washingtonexaminer.biz/web-producers/042815Bernie-sanders-Getty.jpg" width="200" />
<div style="width:200px;"></div>
</div>
<p>Sanders has been a U.S. senator from Vermont since 2007, prior to which he served in the House of Representatives for 16 years. He's now the ranking member of the Senate Budget Committee and chaired the Senate Veterans' Affairs Committee for two years. Technically, Sanders is an independent and has described himself as a democratic socialist in the past. Sanders is the most liberal candidate in the race, having long-championed liberal causes like single-payer healthcare, the expansion of Social Security and opposition to free trade.</p>

<p>Policy Positions:</p>

<p>• Wants to enact a single-payer healthcare system through Medicare for all.</p>

<p>• Wants to make college free for everyone by taxing financial transactions.</p>

<p>• Wants to raise payroll, estate and corporate income taxes.</p>

<p>Status: Declared. Sanders formally announced his campaign in an April 30 speech in front of reporters on Capitol Hill.</p>

<p>Caucus and Primary Results: Iowa (second)</p>

<p>Age on Election Day: 75</p>

<p>Education: University of Chicago (Political Science)</p>

<p>Family: Married (Jane), four children (Levi, Heather, Carina, David)</p>

<p>Birthplace: Brooklyn, N.Y.</p>

<p>Current Residence: Burlington, Vt.</p>

<p>Religion: Judaism</p>

<h4><strong><a href="http://www.washingtonexaminer.com/section/jim-webb" target="_blank">Jim Webb</a></strong></h4>
<p>Status: Dropped out. Webb announced on October 20 that he would withdraw from the Democratic race. He is considering an independent run for president instead. Webb never received more than 6 percent support in a national, Iowa or New Hampshire poll.</p>

<p>T<em>his article was originally published on April 28, 2015 and has been updated regularly.</em></p>
